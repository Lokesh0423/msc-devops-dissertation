# msc-devops-dissertation

**Optimizing Multi-Cloud Environments Through DevOps: A Framework for SMEs**

MSc Information Systems Management — Berlin School of Business and Innovation (2026) — Grade 1.9

## Overview

This dissertation investigates how SMEs can optimize multi-cloud environments by adopting DevOps practices. Based on a quantitative survey of 100 IT and cloud practitioners, it examines the impact of DevOps on performance, scalability, and cost efficiency across AWS and Azure environments.

## Research Questions

- How do DevOps practices influence performance and cost efficiency in SME multi-cloud environments?
- What challenges do SMEs face when adopting multi-cloud strategies and DevOps?
- To what extent can cloud resource optimization strategies improve scalability and security in SMEs?

## Key Findings

- 70% of surveyed SMEs had fully adopted DevOps, mostly in the last 2-3 years
- AWS (47%), Azure (34%), and Google Cloud (19%) were the dominant providers
- Weak but present statistical link between DevOps maturity and cloud performance
- No significant performance variation across cloud providers (ANOVA)
- Skills gap was the biggest barrier, cited by 60% of respondents
- DevOps benefits are tied to organizational maturity, not just technical adoption

## Methodology

- Quantitative research design
- Survey of 100 IT practitioners across manufacturing, retail, and software SMEs
- Statistical analysis: descriptive statistics, correlation, regression, ANOVA
- Tools: Excel, Stats Kingdom
